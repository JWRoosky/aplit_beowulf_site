Beowulf

of Pre-Anglo-Saxon Scandinavian Folklore

Beowulf is an Old English relic of Scandinavian folklore in the style of germanic storytelling. It tells the tale of the great hero Beowulf who travels from his home of Götaland to aid the High King Hrothgar who's great new mead hall is being attacked by a creature called Grendel. Beowulf arrives and kills the beast, as well as it's angered mother who attacks after he kills the monster. Beowulf returns to Götaland where he rules wisely as king for the remainder of his life. However as he nears the end of his life, he goes out to successfully slay a dragon terrorizing his kingdom, and dies shortly thereafter.
